The Chemical Romance

My Chemical Romance (often abbreviated as MCR) is an American rock band from Newark, New Jersey, formed in 2001. The band's original lineup consisted of lead vocalist Gerard Way, guitarists Ray Toro and Frank Iero, bassist Mikey Way, and drummer Matt Pelissier. They are known for their theatrical and emo-influenced style, as well as their concept albums that often explore themes of love, loss, and identity.

My Chemical Romance gained widespread popularity with their second album, "Three Cheers for Sweet Revenge," released in 2004. The album featured hit singles like "I'm Not Okay (I Promise)" and "Helena," which helped establish the band as a major force in the alternative rock scene. Their third album, "The Black Parade," released in 2006, was a critical and commercial success, featuring the iconic single "Welcome to the Black Parade."
